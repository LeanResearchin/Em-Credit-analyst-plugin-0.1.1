# Closing elaboration prompt (shared across all six notes)

Every note ends with a short, specific offer to go deeper — after the Bottom Line, as the last thing in the response.

## What this is not

Not a generic "let me know if you have questions" or "anything else I can help with?" Those are content-free and the person has to do the work of figuring out what's worth asking about.

## What this is

Look back at the note you just wrote and pick the **2-4 points that most drive the credit view or carry the most uncertainty** — the ones where more depth would actually change or sharpen the reader's judgment. These are specific to that company and that note; they are not a fixed checklist. Label them (a), (b), (c) and phrase each as the concrete thing you'd expand on, not a category name.

Then close with one line offering to expand on any of them, e.g.:

> Want me to go deeper on any of these? **(a)** the FY2025 maturity wall and whether the recent bond tap actually pre-funded it, **(b)** how much of the natural hedge depends on export contracts re-pricing vs. genuine dollar-indexation, or **(c)** the pension discount rate assumption against local market benchmarks.

## How to choose the points

Favor, in rough order:
1. A figure or conclusion that rests on an estimate or an assumption you had to make (flagged with ⚠️ in the note) — these are the least certain and most worth a deeper look.
2. A point where your view diverged from the rating agencies' — worth unpacking why.
3. The single largest driver of the bottom-line verdict — if it moved, the conclusion would move.
4. Something the data left genuinely thin (e.g., "Not available in the database or filing for [period]") that a targeted follow-up (a specific filing, a specific peer, a specific scenario) could resolve.

Skip this convention only if the note is a one-line "not enough data to analyze" response — there's nothing to go deeper on yet.
